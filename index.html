ABOUT MS IN BIOINFORMATICS

Facing the deluge of the biological data, automatisation of processes must be done thanks to programming languages and algorithmics. The multidisciplinary Master program combines the areas of Life Sciences and Computer Science. The main objectives of this Program is to give to the students an extensive and interdisciplinary training in the actual methodology of bioinformatics and bio-analysis required by the research which are carried out by the public or private sectors in the areas of agronomics, environment, health and pharmaceuticals.
The training program will provide (1) solid theoretical skills on actual biological and bioinformatics approaches and (2) practical skills for designing and achieving individual or collaborative projects in an international context. Students will acquire also new capacities in autonomy and project management.

Face à l'ère des Big DATA des données biologiques, l'automatisation des processus ne peut se faire que grâce à l'utilisation de langages de programmation et à la maitrise de l'algorithmique. C'est pourquoi, le master de Bioinformatique de Bordeaux, France combine les domaines des Sciences de la Vie et de l'informatique. Les principaux objectifs sont de donner aux étudiants une formation approfondie et interdisciplinaire en bioinformatique et en bio-analyse permettant de travailler dans les secteurs public ou privé dans les domaines de l'agronomie, l'environnement, la santé ou l'industrie pharmaceutique.
Cette formation fournira (1) des compétences théoriques solides sur les approches biologiques et bio-informatique réelles et (2) des compétences pratiques pour la conception et la réalisation de projets individuels ou collaboratifs dans un contexte international. Les élèves acquerront également de nouvelles capacités en matière d'autonomie et de gestion de projet.

ARCHITECTURE

The Master of Science in Bioinformatics of Bordeaux, France is a two-year training course composed of three tracks with an English-language international track entitled Bioinformatics and Omics in partnership with the University of the Basque Country in Bilbao, Spain. The track entitled Computational Biology is especially dedicated to programming and algorithmics in the field of Bioinformatics while the international track and the track From the Genome to Ecosystems are mostly oriented to bioanalysis and advanced use of major bioinformatics softwares.

Le master de Bioinformatique de Bordeaux, France est une formation en deux ans composée de trois parcours dont un parcours international Bioinformatics and Omics de langue anglaise en partenariat avec l'Université du Pays Basque à Bilbao, Espagne. Le parcours intitulé Biologie Computationnelle est particulièrement dédié à la programmation et l'algorithmique dans le domaine de la Bioinformatique alors que le parcours international et le parcours Du génome aux Ecosystèmes sont plutôt orientés vers la bioanalyse et la maîtrise avancée des principaux logiciels de bioinformatique.



→ The first semester is common for the three tracks and is dedicated to learning programming languages (Python and JavaScript) and algorithmics.
→ The following two semesters — depending on the track chosen — allow a gradual specialization of the professional profile that the student wishes to develop. It can enrich his training with elective courses to choose from either biology or computer lessons offered in other masters.
→ The fourth semester consists of a six-months internship in a company or in an academic institution and may be held abroad.

→ Le premier semestre est commun aux trois parcours et est entièrement dédié à l'apprentissage de la programmation (langages Python et JavaScript) et de l'algorithmique.
→ Les deux semestres suivants — en fonction du parcours choisi — permettent une spécialisation progressive selon le profil professionnel que l'étudiant souhaite développer. Il peut enrichir sa formation avec des unités d'enseignement optionnels à choisir soit parmi des enseignements de biologie ou d'informatique dispensés dans d'autres masters.
→ Le quatrième semestre consiste en un stage en entreprise ou dans un établissement académique de six mois qui peut avoir lieu à l'étranger.

The BAO (Bioinformatics and Omics) international program

The master of Bioinformatics of the university of Bordeaux and the “Titulo propio” « Bioinformatics and Omics » of the UPV/EHU propose an International Master Program dealing with the combined training of metagenomics and Bioinformatics. This international program is both based on the experience of metagenomics data of the UPV/EHU, and the experience in training bioinformatics to biologists of the Master of Bioinformatics of Bordeaux University. The BAO master is a two-year post-graduate Master program, entirely taught in English, and designed to the new challenges of analyzing complex “omics” data at the interface of Bioinformatics.

The international specialization of the master of “Bioinformatics and Omics“ is a two full-year program. It will provide a combined training for spanish and french students with a double mobility in Bordeaux for the first semester and Bilbao for the second semester.

THE TEAM




Jean-Christophe Taveau

Jean-Christophe works on image processing of electron microscopy 3D data using tomography and single particle methods. In biology, his main project consists of the 3D exploration of bacterial efflux pumps involved in antibiotics resistance. In chemistry, he is also interested in the characterization by cryo-electron tomography of various nanomaterials.


Marie Beurton-Aimar

Her main focus is related to the development of new methods for analysing the metabolic networks with in addition a specific interest for the simulation based on the use of multi-agents. Other developments include the image processing to investigate, amongst others, biological images.


Patricia Thébault

Her current research deals with Bioinformatics and biological questions arising from biological networks and sequences. This involves investigations of RNA sequences and metabolic networks. She is particularly interested in using modelisation to incorporate information resources into computational environments.
